Queen Bee

Bee Keeping Course
Why Bee Keeping is Important?


All bees are pollinators and pollinators are essential for crops, from fruit and veg to coffee and tea, to be able to thrive.

The Bee population is in danger and Queen Bee's solution is to encourage and teach people about bee keeping and there is now around 1,800 hives through out Northumberland and Scottish Borders.



“If the bee disappeared off the surface of the globe then man would only have four years left to live”

-Albert Einstein

For £30, this 1-day course will contain
Lecture Morning

You will be given an introduction about bees. The basics and essentials, how to take care and how to read their behaviors.

This will give you a good knowledge about them first before starting the course.

There will be a lunch break in between. (Bring your own pack lunch)

Hands-on Afternoon

You will be up-close and personal with the bees and their hive, identify their gender (worker or drone), the eggs and the Queen.

You will also observe and learn about the state of the colony, if your bees are well healthy and learn how to harvest honey.

You will be thought how to properly use the equipment and it will be provided. (You must bring your own boots/wellies)
